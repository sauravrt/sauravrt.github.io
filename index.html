Saurav R Tuladhar

I am a Staff Scientist at ARCON Corporation where I work terminal airspace area automation systems. The automation system ingests reports from multiple sensors invluding: air surveillance radars (ASR), and ADS-B ground stations. The reports contain aircraft state (position and velocity) plus identification meta data. The position and velocity is further processed by a Kalman filter based tracking filter to improve state estimates. The automation system also runs collision avoidance (CA) function to detect potential collision situation in terminal airspace.

Over the last five years, I have worked on multiple projects contributing to the FAA's transition into NexGen technology. Specifically I have worked on

  • Analysis and refining of the Interacting Multiple Model (IMM) filters used to estimate aircraft position and velocity.

  • Implementing reference tracking algorithms as a part of RTCA's efforts to standardize requirements for the radar sensor on board UAVs.

Background

I completed my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering at the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth working with Dr. John R. Buck. As a part of my doctoral research I developed adaptive beamforming algorithms to suppress loud interferers in a training data starved scenarios. Specifically I designed a new technique to improve the minimum variance distortionless response (MVDR) beamformer algorithm. The results are published in this open access paper.
