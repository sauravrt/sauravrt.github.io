# Saurav R Tuladhar
I am a Staff Scientist at [ARCON Corporation](https://www.arcon.com) where I work terminal airspace area automation systems. The automation system ingests data from multiple sensors including: air surveillance radars ([ASR](https://www.faa.gov/air_traffic/technology/asr-11/)), and [ADS-B](https://www.faa.gov/nextgen/programs/adsb/) ground stations. The automation system comprises of data association, state estimation and collision detection subsystems
Over the last five years, I have worked on multiple projects contributing to the FAA's transition into the [NexGen](https://www.faa.gov/nextgen/) technology for airspace automation. Specifically I have worked on  
- Analysis and refining the Interacting Multiple Model (IMM) filters used to estimate aircraft position and velocity.
- Implementing reference correlation and state estimation algorithms as a part of RTCA's efforts to standardize requirements for the radar sensor on board UAVs. 

System tuning and validation involves processing simulate data and examining output data. I extensively use data science tools and techniques to analyze result for system performance evaluation

## Background
I completed my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering at the [University of Massachusetts Dartmouth](https://www.umassd.edu) working with [Dr. John R. Buck](https://www.umassd.edu/directory/jbuck/). As a part of my doctoral research I developed adaptive beamforming algorithms to suppress loud interferers in a training data starved scenarios. Specifically I designed a new technique to improve the minimum variance distortionless response (MVDR) beamformer algorithm. The results are published in this open access [paper](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/8570797).
